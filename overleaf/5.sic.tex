\chapter{Standards and Design Constraints}
\label{ch:standards_and_design_constraints}

This chapter explains the engineering criteria and parameters considered during the development of the real time anxiety detection device. It gives the rationale for the criteria selected, cost analysis/revenue models, and a mapping of the project to complex engineering problems.

\section{Compliance with the Standards}
The system complies with international software, hardware, and communication standards that ensure reliability, alignment with ethics, and compatibility.

\subsection{Software Standards}
IEEE 830-1998 standard for requirements and IEEE 7000-2021 for alignment of AI are used in order to comply with the technical aspect of processing emotional data and ensure consent and safety in terms of data processing.

\subsection{Hardware Standards}
Webcam compatibility is provided due to the USB Video Class (UVC) compliance, and ergonomic ISO 9241-210:2019 standards ensure the non-intrusive nature of using hardware along with software.

\subsection{Communication Standards}
TLS 1.3 and HTTPS secure the data exchange protocol. RESTful API ensures the fast communication between the IDE extension and Python-based engine.

\section{Design Constraints}
Design constraints guide the implementation of the proposed solution to make sure that it works well and is an ethical product.

\subsection{Economic Constraint}
The software should not be expensive for the individual user-developer/student. This constraint limited hardware selection to using standard webcams rather than high-end biometric devices.

\subsection{Environmental Constraint}
The system was developed for the Code::Blocks 20.03 IDE environment. It is supposed to work in a variety of light situations (eye-tracking), without the necessity of having special laboratory environment.

\subsection{Ethical Constraint}
Privacy issues are a must. The software does not store any actual source code or text; it processes only behavioral frequencies (such as pressing of the backspace key).

\subsection{Health and Safety Constraint}
It is necessary that the intervention engine will not intervene too aggressively. Too frequent suggestions may become distracting, thus increasing developers' stress levels.

\subsection{Social Constraints}
The system seeks to address the dearth of mental health resources for developers, fostering a social environment where emotional stability is part and parcel of the work process.

\subsection{Sustainability}
The application makes use of various open-source libraries including TensorFlow and Scikit-learn, allowing for continued maintenance and improvement of the solution without excessive costs related to licensing fees.

\section{Cost Analysis}
The project employs an efficient cost structure. The project budget is 50 to 100 dollars, employing readily available UVC webcams as opposed to expensive biometric devices costing over 1000 dollars.

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{P1 (Depth of Knowledge):} Deep knowledge (K4 \& K5) is required in machine learning as well as engineering design. Multiple areas of knowledge will be involved, such as psychology, and IDE API integration at the lowest level.
    \item \textbf{P2 (Conflicting Requirements):} High depth of information gathering (to get high accuracy of recognition) conflicts with user's privacy needs. On the other hand, real-time information gathering should provide high frequency while not overburdening CPU usage in the IDE.
    \item \textbf{P3 (Depth of Analysis):} The task involves the necessity to develop novel models of behavior (keystroke dynamics) to recognize abstract states (anxiety), which can not be addressed by applying common data analysis methods.
    \item \textbf{P5 (Applicable Codes):} The development process will be strictly bound by a wide variety of international standards, including ISO 27001 (security) and IEEE 7000 (AI ethics).
    \item \textbf{P7 (Inter-dependence):} Interdependency between subsystems is extremely high. Failure of the sensor layer (C++ plugin) results in inability of the intelligence layer (Python ML) to work.
\end{itemize}
